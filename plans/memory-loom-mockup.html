The Night of Falling Embers
Heian Era · Month of the Frost Moon · ~1104 CE
The village was already burning by the time Sukuna realized what was happening. Screams cut through the night as flames consumed wooden homes with hungry indifference. He grabbed her hand — small, calloused from shared labor — and ran. They were almost to the tree line when something struck him from behind. In the chaos of struggle and fire, her hand slipped from his. The last thing he saw was her silver hair catching the firelight before she disappeared into the smoke and darkness. By dawn, he was alone with nothing but ashes and the bitter taste of failure.

The Riverbank Stare Down
Heian Era · Month of the Chrysanthemum · ~1103 CE
She found him there. Not by accident, he would later realize, but because she had been watching. She knelt beside him without asking what happened, without flinching at the blood or the rage radiating off him like heat. She simply began cleaning his wounds. He watched her face — specifically, the small dark mole under her left eye. It caught the moonlight as she leaned closer, a tiny anchor in a face that otherwise seemed too composed for someone touching a monster.

"You're not afraid," he said, his voice rough.

"Should I be? You're just a boy who got beat up. Happens to everyone."
